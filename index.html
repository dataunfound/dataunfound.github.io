Type a URL, and you trigger a structured HTTP request—a digital brushstroke across the network. The server parses this, executes backend logic, and returns an HTTP response (200 OK, or perhaps a 404 art piece). In modern systems, this cycle is heavily optimized through concurrency and caching.

Patch 1.1: Normalisation Trade-offs

Date: 10 March 2026

Normalisation eliminates data redundancy. It's structural purity. But in massive systems requiring raw speed, engineers intentionally denormalise tables. Avoiding complex JOIN operations drastically reduces read latency. It's a fascinating artistic decision between elegance and performance.
